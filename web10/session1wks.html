    Helpful Formulas.

    🔗
    • If the curve is traced out once by the parametric equations \(x=f(t)\) and \(y=g(t)\text{,}\) \(\alpha \leq t \leq \beta\) then we can calculate the area under the curve with the formula \(\displaystyle A=\int_{\alpha}^{\beta}g(t)\ f'(t) \ dt\text{.}\)
      🔗
      🔗
    • If a curve \(C\) is described by the parametric equations \(x=f(t)\text{,}\) \(y=g(t)\text{,}\) \(\alpha \leq t \leq \beta\text{,}\) where \(f'(t)\) and \(g'(t)\) are continuous on \([\alpha,\beta]\) and \(C\) is traversed exactly once as \(t\) increases from \(\alpha\) to \(\beta\text{,}\) then
      🔗
      1. the length, \(L\text{,}\) of \(C\) is given by \(\displaystyle L=\int_{\alpha}^{\beta}\sqrt{\left(\frac{dx}{dt}\right)^{2}+\left( \frac{dy}{dt}\right)^{2}}\ dt\text{.}\)
        🔗
        🔗
      2. the surface area is give by \(\displaystyle S=\int_{\alpha}^{\beta}2\pi r\sqrt{\left(\frac{dx}{dt}\right)^{2}+\left( \frac{dy}{dt}\right)^{2}}\ dt\text{.}\) If the curve is rotated about the \(x-\)axis, then \(g(t)\geq0\) and \(r=y(t)\text{.}\) If the curve is rotated about the \(y-\)axis, then \(f(t)\geq 0\) and \(r=x(t)\text{.}\)
        🔗
        🔗
